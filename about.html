The Team

Gary Libecap

Professor Libecap is the faculty advisor to this project. He is a Corporate Environmental Management professor at the Bren School.

Patricia Song

Contact Patricia

Patricia is the web manager for this site. If anything is broken please contact her.

A lifelong native of Southern California, Patricia is invested in learning about sustainable water management techniques and hopes to be able to help improve the state's water policies and plans. Her main interest lies in urban water resource policies and regulatory changes to improve management strategies. As a master's student at the Bren School specializing in Water Resources Management she hopes to understand the various factors that play into successful water management and the complexities of the economics involved in water use. Patricia earned her B.S in Environmental Systems with an emphasis on ecology from University of California, San Diego in 2014. For 3 summers Patricia has worked in educational outreach at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory with the CloudSat mission. She strongly believes that promoting scientific interest and literacy.

Martin Merz

Contact Martin

Martin Merz is a master’s student at the Bren School pursuing a specialization in Water Resources Management. As part of the Sustainable Water Markets Fellowship Program, he is focusing on how markets can be harnessed to benefit water trusts and state agencies in securing water for instream flows. As research assistant to professor Gary Libecap, he is working on a Strategic Environmental Research Initiative (SERI) project regarding dams in the United States, and on the Multicampus Research Programs and Initiatives (MRPI) research on the potential for environmental markets in California. He is part of the Bren Environmental Justice Club, the Student-Faculty Social Committee, and founded the Bren Spanish Conversation Club. Martin holds a BA in environmental studies and ecology and evolutionary biology, with a minor in Geography, from the University of Colorado, Boulder, where, as a research assistant, he studied the effects of trout introductions on high-elevation lakes.

Adriel Leon

Contact Adriel

Adriel Leon is a master’s student specializing in water resources management at the Bren School of Environmental Science & Management. He comes to the Bren School after working for three years in the healthcare industry. During his undergraduate, he worked as a research assistant on multiple projects covering topics from aquaculture development for Sea Urchins to lead (Pb) concentrations in soils in Baltimore’s rural and urban parks. His interest in environmental science took off when he worked as a research assistant to develop a native plant species palette for the Green Roofs in Los Angeles. At the Bren School, he plans to build upon his research background to analyze current water supply challenges in the Western United States. Adriel graduated from Loyola Marymount University in 2010 with a B.S. in Biology.

Lillian Burns

Contact Lillian

Lilian graduated with her Bachelor's in Chemistry from Chapman University in 2012. During her undergraduate degree she completed research for Dr Catherine Clark,an internship with Orange County Watersheds Special Monitoring Programs Unit, and was an active member of the Chapman community. After graduating, Lillian became the lab manager for Dr. Chris Kim's environmental geochemistry laboratory while also completing research. Her research focused on the fate and transport of metal pollutants from historic gold mines in California. Lillian now attends the Bren school where she is specializing in Pollution Prevention and Remediation. She completed research for Dr Arturo Keller during her first two quarters and currently is interning as a consultant for the Department of Toxic Substance Control. Through her work on the group project she hopes to gain a better knowledge of water trusts and environmental markets' role in restoring instream flow.She is acting as the group's financial manager.
